Irish-Indigenous Interactions

A digital exploration of historical connections between Ireland and Indigenous nations.

Introduction

In 1847, during the height of the Great Famine in Ireland, the Choctaw Nation, located in what is now Oklahoma, sent a remarkable donation to aid those suffering thousands of miles away. This gesture of solidarity, along with other moments of exchange, activism, and cultural connection between Irish and Indigenous peoples, reveals a shared history of resilience, empathy, and transatlantic relationships. This digital project explores these interactions through maps, timelines, and stories, highlighting the people, events, and themes that shaped this chapter of history.
