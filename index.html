Project Overview

In 1847, during the height of the Great Famine in Ireland, the Choctaw Nation, located in what is now Oklahoma, sent a remarkable donation to aid those suffering thousands of miles away. This gesture of solidarity, along with other moments of exchange, activism, and cultural connection between Irish and Indigenous peoples, reveals a shared history of resilence, empathy, and transatlantic relationships.

This digital project explores these interactions through maps, timelines, and stories, highlighting the people, events, and themes that shaped this chapter of history.

Research Question

How did Irish and Indigenous peoples interact and support each other across the Atlantic, and what does this reveal about shared experiences of resilience and cultural exchange?

Why It Matters

Understanding the interactions between Irish and Indigenous peoples provides insight into the complexities of transatlantic relationships and the ways in which different communities have supported each other during times of crisis. This project highlights the resilience and cultural exchange that have shaped both histories.
