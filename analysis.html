Timeline of Key Events

Introduction

Ireland & Indigenous Worlds

Irish and Indigenous histories intersect through colonialism, migration, and moments of solidarity that crossed the Atlantic world.

1845–1852

Great Irish Famine

A catastrophic famine devastated Ireland, leading to mass death and emigration. British colonial policies exacerbated suffering.

1830s

Indian Removal & Aftermath

The Choctaw Nation had recently endured forced removal during the Trail of Tears, suffering immense loss and displacement.

1847

Choctaw Donation

Despite their own hardships, the Choctaw Nation raised funds for Irish famine relief— a powerful act of transnational solidarity.

1840s–1860s

Irish Migration to North America

Millions of Irish emigrated, many settling in the United States and becoming part of expanding settler colonial systems.

19th Century

Colonial Entanglements

Irish immigrants navigated complex roles—both marginalized and complicit in the displacement of Indigenous peoples.

20th Century

Memory & Narrative

Public memory increasingly emphasized Irish suffering while often overlooking Indigenous histories and shared colonial contexts.

2017

Kindred Spirits Memorial

The Kindred Spirits sculpture in Ireland commemorates the Choctaw donation, symbolizing enduring connections.

2020

Irish Donation to Navajo & Hopi Nations

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Irish people raised funds for Navajo and Hopi communities, reciprocating the Choctaw Nation’s 1847 generosity.

Today

Ongoing Connections

Irish–Indigenous relationships continue to be revisited through scholarship, memorials, and activism.
