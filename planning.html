Irish–Indigenous Interactions

Home Planning Findings Sustainability

Project Overview

This digital history project explores the historical and contemporary connections between Ireland and Indigenous nations, with a particular focus on the 1847 Choctaw donation during the Great Irish Famine. While this moment serves as an entry point, the project expands outward to examine broader themes of colonialism, migration, and memory.

User Profile

This project is designed for a graduate-level student or general museum-style web visitor who is interested in colonial history, transnational solidarity, and digital storytelling. The user will also prefer visual aids and guided interpretation rather than dense academic text.

Research Questions

  • How did the Choctaw donation of 1847 shape Irish–Indigenous relationships?
  • In what ways do Irish and Indigenous histories intersect through colonialism and displacement?
  • How have these interactions been remembered and commemorated over time?
  • How can digital tools help visualize and interpret these connections?

Methodology

Digital Mapping

ArcGIS was used to visualize geographic connections between Ireland and Indigenous nations. Mapping allowed for spatial analysis of migration routes, sites of displacement, and locations of commemoration.

Timeline Construction

A chronological timeline was developed to situate key events within broader historical contexts, connecting Indigenous removal, the Irish famine, and later acts of remembrance.

Primary Source Analysis

The project incorporates newspapers, historical accounts, and government documents to provide insight into contemporary understandings of these events.

Design & Structure

The website was designed to function as a digital exhibit, guiding users through a narrative experience. The homepage introduces the topic, while the findings section integrates timeline, map, and archival materials to support interpretation.

A clean, minimalist design was chosen to prioritize accessibility, while maintaining a museum-like aesthetic.

Page Design Reasoning

Home Page

The home page was designed to immediately orient visitors with a clear narrative entry point into Irish–Indigenous connections. It prioritizes engagement and overview context to guide users into exploring deeper analytical sections.

Findings Page

The findings page reveals primary analysis through maps, timelines, and archival sources. It is structured to encourage exploratory learning, matching the intended user profile of a guided but self-directed learner.

Sustainability Page

This page emphasizes reflection on digital preservation and ethical storytelling. It is designed to guide users toward thinking critically about how historical narratives are maintained and shared online.

Design Changes from Sketch to Build

Several changes were made between the sketching phase and the final build, including simplifying navigation structure, reducing visual clutter for readability, and integrating timeline/map elements directly into the findings page rather than separating them across multiple pages.

Challenges & Limitations

  • Balancing narrative storytelling with analytical depth
  • Representing complex colonial relationships without oversimplification
  • Locating accessible and reliable primary sources
  • Integrating digital tools (ArcGIS, HTML) into a cohesive experience
