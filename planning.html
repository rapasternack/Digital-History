Planning

Research design, methodology, and project development

Project Overview

This digital history project explores the historical and contemporary connections between Ireland and Indigenous nations, with a particular focus on the 1847 Choctaw donation during the Great Irish Famine. While this moment serves as an entry point, the project expands outward to examine broader themes of colonialism, migration, and memory.

Research Questions

  • How did the Choctaw donation of 1847 shape Irish–Indigenous relationships?
  • In what ways do Irish and Indigenous histories intersect through colonialism and displacement?
  • How have these interactions been remembered and commemorated over time?
  • How can digital tools help visualize and interpret these connections?

Methodology

Digital Mapping

ArcGIS StoryMaps was used to visualize geographic connections between Ireland and Indigenous nations. Mapping allowed for spatial analysis of migration routes, sites of displacement, and locations of commemoration.

Timeline Construction

A chronological timeline was developed to situate key events within broader historical contexts, connecting Indigenous removal, the Irish famine, and later acts of remembrance.

Primary Source Analysis

The project incorporates newspapers, historical accounts, and government documents to provide insight into contemporary understandings of these events.

Design & Structure

The website was designed to function as a digital exhibit, guiding users through a narrative experience. The homepage introduces the topic, while the findings section integrates timeline, map, and archival materials to support interpretation.

A clean, minimalist design was chosen to prioritize readability and accessibility, while maintaining a professional and museum-like aesthetic.

Challenges & Limitations

  • Balancing narrative storytelling with analytical depth
  • Representing complex colonial relationships without oversimplification
  • Locating accessible and reliable primary sources
  • Integrating digital tools (ArcGIS, HTML) into a cohesive experience
